Demetra Vagias, MD, ND 
Dr. Vagias’ love of the sciences was apparent from an early age. It was inevitable that she pursue her passion – helping people find their way to health. So she embarked on her educational journey to fulfill her life’s mission. Dr. Vagias obtained a bachelor's degree in biology, a doctorate degree in medicine and ultimately residency training in family practice. This, she thought, would afford her the experience she needed to show people how to reclaim their health. While in residency, her expected results always fell short, and with each passing day Dr. Vagias questioned the conventional medical approach to addressing health issues. Searching for insight into the plight at hand, she revisited her Hippocratic oath, hoping for answers. Upon careful examination, Dr. Vagias soon realized conventional medical care and the oath she swore to uphold were in direct conflict. With these revelations, Dr. Vagias resigned from her medical position and re-examined her career choice. Read the Chicago Wellness magazine article Dr. Vagias wrote about the road which led her to naturopathic medicine.

While obtaining her doctorate in natural medicine, Dr. Vagias turned to her Greek roots and the medical practices of Hippocrates ("father of medicine") for inspiration and guidance. She searched for deeper insight into healing and living life more connected to spirit and nature. Through her observations and studies, Dr. Vagias learned to combine the art and science of healing. She discovered an incredible world of science which exists – mind and body working together to heal the human being! This was her "eureka" moment …

Dr. Vagias now practices natural medicine exclusively, offering a deeper and lasting path to health for anyone who seeks it. She is one of very few physicians in the country to hold dual doctorate degrees. This places her in a unique position to offer her expertise from both disciplines to optimize wellness.

Dr. Vagias' philosophy: "Health and disease come from a complex interaction of physical, emotional, dietary, genetic, environmental, lifestyle and other factors. My attention is on the body as a whole, and my role in the healing process is to support the body as it restores balance and health. Since attaining health is a personal journey requiring commitment and a work in progress, my other role is as teacher ... The truth is simple, therefore, the path to health and healing must be simple …“
